Unity Platformer

For my third project I wanted to work with a classic platform of game development, Unity. I took an old platformer with just basic movement, and decided to add some more unique mechanics. Some notes:

  • I learned that my need to make things look and feel good was wasting a large amount of what limited time I had. I redeisgned my old scripts and sprites, but focused for nearly three days on player movement. As a game player myself, I felt movement needed to feel near perfect or it would ruin the whole game.I had to shelf this idealogy and cut corners on a time constraint.
  • Never in my life do I like to make things easy for myself and this was no different. I had big ambitions knowing I wasn't going to have the time to do it all, but this time it was jow much I wanted mechanically. I forgot about level design, which took up 2 days of panicked and rushed set up after I perfected my testing level.
  • The point of testing myself and pushing my limits and sanity are not because it is required. I do this because without pressure I would feel like I didn't learn or improve. The goal of these assignments was to experience a mimic of an internship, and if I went easy on myself I would miss out on imporant lessons.
  • I learned about priority and flexability in time constraints. Nothing majorly new, but this was more refined. I was my own boss so my personal priorities were being tested over my objective ones. Instead of trying to force one system of jumping or ground detection to work for every mechanic, I gritted my teeth and learned how to code seperate systems for these mechanics for their indvidual purpose. I also was forced to set down my need to make things look nice, my job isn't the art in the grand scheme. These lessons, while small, are crucial.
